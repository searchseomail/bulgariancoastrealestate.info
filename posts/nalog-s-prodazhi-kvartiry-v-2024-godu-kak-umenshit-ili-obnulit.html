Налог с продажи квартиры в 2024 году: как уменьшить или обнулить

Emily Johnson 13.04.2026 • Reading time: 6 min

Налог с продажи квартиры. Как законно уменьшить сумму или не платить вообще?

Selling real estate is a significant financial event, and for many, the prospect of paying a 13% tax on the transaction comes as an unpleasant surprise. In 2024, the Russian tax legislation remains relatively stable, yet it is full of nuances that can save you a substantial amount of money if you plan your sale correctly. Understanding the rules regarding the "minimum tenure period" and deductible expenses is crucial for every property owner.

If you are planning to sell your property, it is vital to approach the process with the same level of due diligence as when you were buying it. Just as you would use a comprehensive checklist to verify a secondary market property, you must verify your tax status before signing the purchase agreement. Failing to do so can lead to unexpected tax liabilities that could eat into your profit.

Understanding the Minimum Tenure Period

The most effective way to avoid paying tax on the sale of an apartment is to hold the property for a specific period, known as the "minimum tenure." Once this period has passed, the income from the sale is completely exempt from personal income tax (NDFL). In most cases, this period is five years. However, there are exceptions where the period is reduced to three years:

  • The property was acquired through inheritance or as a gift from a close relative.
  • The property was privatized.
  • The property was received under a lifelong maintenance contract.
  • The property is your "sole residential property" (meaning you own no other residential real estate at the time of the sale).

Tax Calculation Methods: How to Reduce the Burden

If you have owned the apartment for less than the minimum tenure period, you are legally required to declare the income. However, you have two primary methods to reduce your tax base: the "fixed deduction" or the "income minus expenses" calculation.

"Tax optimization is not tax evasion. It is the legal application of deductions and credits provided by the state to reduce your fiscal burden based on your specific life circumstances."

The fixed deduction allows you to subtract 1 million rubles from the sale price of the apartment. You only pay the 13% tax on the remaining amount. This is particularly useful if you do not have documents proving your initial purchase costs. Alternatively, if you have proof of the original purchase price (contract, receipts, bank statements), you can subtract those expenses from the sale price and pay tax only on the profit (the difference between the buy and sell price). If you bought the apartment as an investment, remember to weigh these options against other factors, such as whether you are choosing between apartments or residential flats, as their tax status can differ.

Comparison of Tax Reduction Strategies

Method Applicability Best Used When
Fixed Deduction Any property You have no documents for initial expenses.
Income Minus Expenses Documented purchases The profit margin is small or expenses were high.
Full Exemption After min. tenure You have held the property for 3 or 5 years.

Additional Considerations: Mortgages and Capital

It is important to note that if you are selling a property that was purchased using maternity capital, there are specific legal requirements regarding the allocation of shares to children. These complications often arise later, so it is worth reading about the pitfalls of selling properties involving maternity capital to ensure you are not violating the rights of minors, which could lead to severe legal issues with the guardianship authorities.

Finally, always remember to file your tax return (Form 3-NDFL) by April 30th of the year following the sale. Even if you owe zero tax, if you sold the property before the minimum tenure ended, you are still required to submit the declaration to the Federal Tax Service. Failure to do so can result in fines, regardless of whether a tax was actually due.

Frequently Asked Questions

What happens if I sell the apartment for the same price I bought it for?
Even if you made no profit, you are still required to file a tax return. However, if you use the "income minus expenses" method, your tax base will be zero, and therefore, you will not owe any tax.
Can I use the fixed deduction if I sold two apartments in one year?
Yes, but the 1 million ruble deduction is the total limit per year for all residential properties sold. You cannot apply 1 million to each apartment separately.
Does the tax apply to non-residents of Russia?
Yes, and the tax rate for non-residents is significantly higher (30%). Furthermore, non-residents generally cannot apply the standard tax deductions available to tax residents of the Russian Federation.
When does the tenure period start?
The period typically starts from the date of state registration of ownership, which is recorded in the Unified State Register of Real Estate (EGRN).
