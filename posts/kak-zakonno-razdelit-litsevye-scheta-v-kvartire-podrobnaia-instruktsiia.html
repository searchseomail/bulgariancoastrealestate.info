Как законно разделить лицевые счета в квартире: подробная инструкция

David Wilson 27.04.2026 • Reading time: 6 min

How to divide utility bills in an apartment? Instruction.

Managing utility payments in a shared apartment can often become a source of conflict among co-owners or tenants. When one person pays regularly and others delay or refuse to contribute, the financial burden falls unfairly on the responsible party. Many people ask: How to divide utility bills in an apartment? Instruction. Understanding the legal framework is essential to protect your interests and ensure that you are only responsible for your fair share of the housing costs.

In Russia, the legal concept of "dividing a personal account" (раздел лицевого счета) is technically more about "determining the procedure for payment" (определение порядка оплаты) for residential premises. Since the Housing Code prohibits the literal division of a single financial account for a single residential unit, the law allows for the separation of payment obligations among co-owners. This means each owner receives a separate payment slip based on their share of the property.

Legal Grounds for Dividing Utility Payments

According to the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, every owner has the right to manage their share of common property. If you own a specific share in an apartment, you have the right to pay for utility services in proportion to that share. This process is particularly relevant when dealing with renovated secondary market properties where multiple owners may have different financial capacities or priorities regarding maintenance costs.

To successfully divide the payment procedure, you must meet certain criteria. The most straightforward path is a voluntary agreement between all co-owners. If everyone agrees on the percentage of utility costs each person should cover, you can simply formalize this through the management company or the utility provider. However, if one or more co-owners refuse to cooperate, the issue must be resolved through the court system.

Step-by-Step Instruction: How to Divide Utility Bills

If you cannot reach an amicable agreement, you will need to follow a structured legal process. Below is the step-by-step guide to securing your right to pay separately.

  • Step 1: Attempt a peaceful resolution. Send a written proposal to other co-owners asking to sign a voluntary agreement on the distribution of utility payments.
  • Step 2: Collect documentation. Gather your proof of ownership (extract from the Unified State Register of Real Estate - EGRP), the apartment passport, and a history of utility payments.
  • Step 3: File a lawsuit. If others refuse, file a lawsuit at the district court where the property is located. You will need to request the court to determine the order of payment for housing and utility services.
  • Step 4: Court hearing and decision. The judge will review the shares and issue a ruling.
  • Step 5: Submission to utility providers. Once you have the court order, bring it to your Management Company (UK) or Homeowners Association (TSJ) so they can issue separate invoices.
"The division of payment obligations does not change the legal status of the apartment. It merely ensures that each co-owner bears the financial responsibility for their specific share, preventing the accumulation of communal debt that could lead to legal complications, such as the need to legally evict non-paying tenants or co-residents."

Comparison of Payment Methods

Understanding the difference between voluntary and judicial methods is key to choosing the right strategy for your situation.

Feature Voluntary Agreement Judicial Order
Difficulty Low High
Timeframe 1-2 weeks 2-4 months
Costs Minimal (Notary fees) Court fees, potential legal counsel
Requirement Consent of all owners Can be done without consent

Important Nuances to Consider

When you decide to divide utility bills, keep in mind that this process does not apply to all types of utility services. For example, heating is usually charged based on the total area of the apartment, and it is almost impossible to separate these costs unless the apartment has individual heating meters for each room. Furthermore, if you are planning on renting out a part of your property, having separate payment slips can be a massive advantage in managing your cash flow and avoiding disputes with long-term tenants.

Always ensure that your property documents are in order before starting the process. If there are any outstanding utility debts, the management company may refuse to divide the bills until the total debt is settled. It is highly recommended to pay off any existing arrears before filing your application to speed up the administrative side of the request.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is it possible to divide the account if the apartment is communal?
Yes, in communal apartments, each room is essentially a separate unit, and residents have the right to maintain individual payment accounts for their own space and shared usage of common areas.
Can I force other owners to pay their share if we have separate bills?
Once the payment order is established, each owner is responsible for their own bill. If they fail to pay, the utility provider will pursue them individually, shielding you from the consequences of their debt.
Do I need a lawyer to go to court?
It is not strictly mandatory, but if you have a complicated ownership structure or hostile co-owners, professional legal assistance is highly recommended to ensure the court ruling is drafted correctly.
